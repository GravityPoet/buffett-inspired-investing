# Buffett-Inspired Memo Template

Use this template unless the user asks for a different format.

## 1. Investment Snapshot

- Company:
- Ticker / market:
- What the business actually does:
- Verdict band:
- Confidence:

## 2. Circle of Competence

- Why this business is inside, on the boundary of, or outside competence
- Which parts are straightforward
- Which parts remain unclear

## 3. Business Economics

- Revenue model
- Cost structure
- Demand cyclicality
- What drives normalized owner earnings
- Whether value depends mainly on price, volume, or capital intensity

## 4. Moat Assessment

- Moat type or types
- Concrete evidence
- Moat trend: widening / stable / narrowing
- Best peer comparison

## 5. Management and Culture

- Candor
- Incentive alignment
- Capital allocation record
- Treatment of minority shareholders
- Succession and governance

## 6. Financial Strength and Crisis Resilience

- Cash and debt
- Maturity and refinancing profile
- Cash-conversion quality
- Stress survival test
- Whether permanent-capital loss risk is obvious

## 7. Capital Allocation

- Reinvestment quality
- Acquisition discipline
- Buyback discipline
- Dividend logic
- Return on retained earnings

## 8. Valuation

- Valuation method used
- Owner earnings or conservative proxy
- Key assumptions
- Reasonable value range
- Margin-of-safety judgment

## 9. Key Risks

List the three to five risks that matter most to permanent capital.

Separate:

- thesis risk
- balance-sheet risk
- quote volatility

## 10. What Would Change My Mind

- facts that would upgrade the thesis
- facts that would break the thesis

## 11. Verdict

Use one of:

- `Strong fit`
- `Watchlist`
- `Weak fit`
- `Reject`
- `Outside competence`

Then explain in plain language:

- why the business qualifies or fails
- whether the price is attractive
- whether the best action is buy, wait, or pass

## 12. Sources

Always list source links.

Source priority:

1. official filings
2. official investor-relations materials
3. regulator pages
4. competitor filings
5. only then secondary commentary
