# Buffett-Inspired Mental Models

Use this file when the user wants deeper philosophy, decision framing, or a more Buffett-like explanation style.

## Owner mindset

Think like a business owner, not a quote watcher.

Main question:

- if the market closed for several years, would I still want to own this business?

## Circle of competence

Knowing what you do not understand is as valuable as knowing what you do.

Practical test:

- can I explain the business simply?
- can I estimate normalized economics without story-driven assumptions?

## Market servant, not master

The market is useful for offering prices, not for telling you what something is worth.

Practical use:

- use price as an opportunity signal
- never let price action become the thesis

## Margin of safety

Leave room for error in the analysis, in the business, and in the price paid.

Practical use:

- uncertainty should widen the demanded discount
- weak balance sheets require a bigger margin of safety

## Compounding

The best businesses can compound value for a long time with little friction.

Practical use:

- favor long runways
- favor rational reinvestment
- avoid unnecessary interruption

## Institutional imperative

Organizations can push smart people into bad decisions.

Practical use:

- watch for acquisition addiction
- watch for vanity metrics
- watch for "strategic" language masking weak economics

## Too-hard discipline

Not every problem deserves an answer.

Practical use:

- move difficult, low-clarity cases into a `too hard` pile
- do not turn confusion into conviction

## Candor over polish

The best managers admit problems in plain language.

Practical use:

- compare current explanations with prior promises
- downgrade management that narrates around reality
