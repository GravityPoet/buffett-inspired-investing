# Industry Playbooks

Different industries require different questions.

Use this file after the base framework, not before it.

## Insurance

What matters most:

- underwriting discipline
- combined ratio
- float quality and cost
- reserve conservatism
- investment discipline

Questions:

- is float low-cost or truly value-destructive?
- does management sacrifice underwriting quality for premium growth?
- are reserves conservative or optimistic?
- is investment risk being taken to hide weak underwriting?

## Banking

What matters most:

- deposit quality
- underwriting discipline
- leverage
- credit culture
- asset quality visibility

Questions:

- is the loan book understandable?
- does the bank rely on hot funding?
- can the balance sheet survive a real credit shock?
- are management incentives aligned with prudence or loan growth?

## Consumer brands and habitual purchases

What matters most:

- repeat purchase behavior
- pricing power
- distribution advantage
- low capital intensity

Questions:

- can the company raise prices without damaging volume materially?
- does the brand behave like habit or merely like fashion?
- is cash conversion consistently strong?

## Platforms and software

Buffett-style caution is higher here because change can be faster.

What matters most:

- switching costs
- network effects
- customer concentration
- reinvestment quality
- durability of economics under technological change

Questions:

- are economics durable or just temporarily boosted by a cycle?
- is retention structural or incentive-driven?
- can the moat be described without hype words?

## Capital-intensive cyclicals

Examples:

- airlines
- shipping
- commodity producers
- heavy manufacturing with weak differentiation

Default stance:

- skepticism first

Questions:

- is there any real moat, or just favorable pricing today?
- what do normalized returns look like across a full cycle?
- can the company survive when demand and pricing both weaken?

## Retail and distribution

What matters most:

- local density or distribution edge
- inventory discipline
- pricing power or cost advantage
- management discipline

Questions:

- is the retailer a true low-cost operator?
- is growth destroying returns on capital?
- are store-level economics still attractive?

## Holding companies and conglomerates

What matters most:

- capital allocation at the parent level
- transparency
- incentive structure
- look-through earnings
- discount to conservative intrinsic value

Questions:

- is management compounding per-share value or just empire-building?
- are subsidiaries high-quality, or is the structure hiding weak economics?
- can look-through value be estimated with discipline?
